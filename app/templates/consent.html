Informed Consent Disclosure

TrueHair AI Hairstyle Visualization Study

Colby College — Department of Computer Science — CS422

Please read carefully before participating
This is an IRB-approved research study. Your participation is entirely voluntary.
1

Purpose of This Research

You are being invited to participate in a research study conducted by students in CS422 at Colby College, under the supervision of Prof. Naser Al Madi. The purpose of this study is to investigate whether AI-powered personalized hairstyle recommendations improve user engagement and perceived output quality on the TrueHair AI platform compared to a standard hairstyle catalog.

Participation is entirely voluntary. Please read this disclosure carefully before deciding whether to participate.

3

What You Will Be Asked To Do

If you agree to participate, you will:

  • Access the TrueHair AI web application at https://truehair-ai-c77120f7a186.herokuapp.com/.
  • Be randomly assigned to one of two study conditions:
    • a control group that browses a catalog of predefined hairstyles, or
    • an experimental group that receives AI-powered personalized hairstyle recommendations highlighted within the same full catalog, with AI-generated reasoning for each recommendation.
  • Upload a front-facing portrait photograph of yourself for hairstyle visualization.
  • Generate one or more hairstyle visualizations using the interface available to your assigned group.
  • Rate each AI-generated visualization on a 1-5 star scale.
  • Complete the session at your own pace. Expected duration is approximately 10-15 minutes.

The platform will automatically record your session duration, the number of visualizations you generate, the hairstyles you select, your star ratings, and (for the experimental group) whether each hairstyle you selected was AI-recommended. You will not be asked to complete any additional surveys or interviews.

4

Information We Will and Will Not Collect

This is a fully anonymous research study. The research team will not collect any personally identifiable information about you.

Specifically, we will NOT collect
  • Your name
  • Your email address
  • Any account or login information
  • Your IP address (stripped from server logs)
  • Any persistent record of your photograph or generated visualizations
We WILL record
  • An anonymous session identifier (a random code with no link to your identity)
  • Which experimental condition you were assigned to
  • Which hairstyles you visualized (catalog identifiers only), and whether each was AI-recommended (experimental group only)
  • Your 1-5 star ratings of each visualization
  • How long you spent on the platform
  • How many visualizations you generated

Your photograph is required to generate the hairstyle visualization, but it is processed in server memory only and is never saved to any database, cloud storage, or log file. Once your visualization is generated, your photograph and the generated image are immediately discarded from the server.

5

Use of AI Technology and Third-Party Processing

This study uses Google's Gemini models via the Vertex AI API to generate hairstyle visualizations and personalized recommendations from your uploaded photograph. By participating, you acknowledge that your photograph will be transmitted to Google's API servers for processing. The following protections apply:

  • Google's paid-tier terms of service prohibit using API request data to train Google's AI models.
  • Google retains API request data for up to 90 days for abuse monitoring before automatic deletion (see Google Cloud's abuse monitoring policy: https://cloud.google.com/vertex-ai/generative-ai/docs/learn/abuse-monitoring). The research team has taken steps to achieve Zero Data Retention (ZDR) as documented in Google Cloud's data governance policy (https://cloud.google.com/vertex-ai/generative-ai/docs/data-governance):
    data caching has been disabled so your photo is not stored beyond the immediate request, and an exception from prompt logging for abuse monitoring has been applied for, which upon approval will ensure your photo is not logged at all.
  • The API call carries no information about you (no name, no email, no account identifier). Google has no means of associating the photograph with you as an individual.

If you have concerns about Google's handling of API data, you can review Google Cloud's data governance policy at https://cloud.google.com/vertex-ai/generative-ai/docs/data-governance.

6

Potential Risks

The risks associated with this study are minimal:

  • Minor emotional discomfort related to self-image when viewing AI-generated visualizations of yourself with different hairstyles.
  • Minimal privacy considerations associated with briefly transmitting your photograph to Google's Vertex AI API. Mitigations are described in Section 5 above.
  • Please upload only photographs of yourself, or photographs of others for which you have explicit permission from the person depicted. Do not upload images of others without their consent.
7

Potential Benefits

You may personally benefit from exploring hairstyle options through the platform. More broadly, this study contributes to research on AI personalization and the design of human-centered AI systems. There is no direct financial compensation for participation.

8

Voluntary Participation and Right to Withdraw

Your participation in this study is completely voluntary. You may withdraw at any time during your session by simply closing the browser window. No penalty or consequence will result from withdrawing.

Important: Because this study is fully anonymous and we collect no information that could identify you, we cannot identify your specific data within our records after your session ends. As a result, we are unable to honor requests to delete data after the fact, because we have no way to determine which records belong to you. If you do not wish your data to be included in the study, please withdraw before completing the session by closing the browser.

9

Confidentiality

Confidentiality is preserved by design: the research team has no technical means to identify any participant. No identifying information will appear in any published reports, presentations, or academic work, because no identifying information has ever been collected. All published findings will use anonymous, aggregate data only.

10

Eligibility

You are eligible to participate if you are 18 years of age or older. This study does not restrict participation based on gender, race, ethnicity, or any other demographic characteristic.

11

  • You are 18 years of age or older.
