Privacy Notice

Last updated: April 2026

Our Commitment to Privacy

TrueHair.ai is an anonymous research study conducted by students and faculty at Colby College, reviewed and approved by the Colby College Institutional Review Board (IRB). This study does not create accounts, collect names or email addresses, or retain the photographs you upload. This notice describes exactly what we do and do not record, so you can read it alongside the consent disclosure you review at the start of a session.

What We Collect (and What We Don't)

This is an anonymous research study. We have taken deliberate steps to avoid collecting personally identifiable information.

We do NOT collect:
  • Your name, email address, or phone number
  • Any account or login information (there is no account system)
  • Your IP address (stripped from application logs)
  • A stored copy of your uploaded photograph (held in server memory only during AI processing, then discarded)
  • A stored copy of the AI-generated hairstyle visualizations (streamed to your browser, never saved on our servers)
We DO record:
  • An anonymous session ID (a random code, not linked to your identity)
  • Which experimental condition you were assigned to
  • Which hairstyles you selected (catalog IDs only)
  • Your 1–5 star ratings of generated visualizations
  • How long your session lasted and how many visualizations you generated
  • The timestamp of your click on “I Agree”

How Your Data is Used

The anonymous session records listed above are used solely for academic research — to evaluate how participants interact with AI-generated hairstyle recommendations and to report aggregate findings. Your data is never sold, shared with advertisers, or used for any commercial purpose.

Third-Party AI Processing

Your uploaded photograph is transmitted to Google’s Gemini models via the Vertex AI API to generate hairstyle visualizations and recommendations. Google’s paid-tier terms of service prohibit using API request data to train Google’s AI models.

We have configured Zero Data Retention (ZDR) settings on the Vertex AI project: data caching is disabled, and an abuse-monitoring logging exception has been applied for (see Google Cloud’s data governance policy).

API calls to Google carry no information about you (no name, email, or account identifier). Google has no means of associating a photograph with you as an individual.

Can I remove my data after participating?

Because this study is fully anonymous, we cannot identify which records belong to any specific participant after a session ends. As a result, we are unable to honor requests to delete specific data after the fact.

If you do not want your data to be included, please close the browser window before completing your session. If you have concerns, you can contact the research team or the Colby IRB using the information below.
